Welcome, I'm Maeva.

I am a PhD student in Alfred Spormann's lab at Stanford University. My research focuses on the metabolism of slow-growing model microorganisms, in order to better understand how deep-subsurface organisms can live for hundreds to thousands of years. .

When I am not running experiments in the lab, I am exploring old and new datasets, and designing visualisations. Have a look at the projects I've worked on.

Before joining the Spormann lab, I graduated from the Ecole Centrale Paris, one of the top French Engineering School, in 2012, and obtained a M.S in Environmental Engineering from Stanford University in 2013.
